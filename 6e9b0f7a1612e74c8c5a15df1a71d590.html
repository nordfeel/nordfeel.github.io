6 natural foods that are useful for constipation

6 natural foods that are useful for constipation

1. Sea buckthorn California.

Sea buckthorn is a popular laxative plant. Short-term use is usually well tolerated, but may cause abdominal pain or disturbance electrolytic balance. Prolonged use can lead to liver damage ranging from mild to acute liver failure.

2. Indian plantain.

Indian plantain is a natural laxative with fiber. It is often used for chronic constipation and can be combined with other laxatives, both natural and synthetic. May cause an allergic reaction, nausea, abdominal pain or vomiting.

3. Rhubarb.

Although this plant is mainly known as a baking ingredient, it is also used to treat constipation.

Rhubarb has a laxative effect, but one study found that it also has an Antidiarrhoeal effect due to its tannin content. Therefore, rhubarb should only be used for short-term use for constipation.

4. Senna.

The plant is used to treat constipation and to cleanse the intestines before some medical procedure. It is recommended to use only short-term and in recommended doses. Prolonged use and high doses can cause liver damage.

5. Red elm.

Red elm stimulates nerve endings in the gastrointestinal tract, which leads to the production of mucus and relieves constipation.

A limited study was conducted to determine long-term effects. Using the plant can reduce the absorption of certain medications if taken at the same time as them.

6. Dill.

Its seeds are used to eliminate problems such as indigestion stomach pain, bloating, constipation, and irritable bowel syndrome, as they activate the smooth muscle motility of the digestive tract.
