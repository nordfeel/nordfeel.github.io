Products that cause unpleasant body odor

Body odor is a very sensitive topic for most of us, and there is hardly a person who wants to smell unpleasant, especially in society. We spend a lot of money on various deodorants, perfume and other cosmetics to prevent excessive sweating and bad smell.

Sweating is a completely normal, necessary reaction of the body, which protects against overheating and helps to remove harmful toxins. Frequent use of deodorants clogs the skin's pores in the most important areas areas such as the armpits and this can lead to problems. Instead, you can focus on natural ways to reduce sweating and unpleasant odors.

To achieve this goal, you should avoid frequent intake of some of the following products, which can cause unpleasant odors bodies.

Dark green leafy vegetables.

Cabbage and broccoli are extremely healthy vegetables, and you should not completely abandon them. However, due to the increased content of sulfur in them, when consumed in large quantities, they provoke a bad smell. One of the ways to deal with this unpleasant effect - before using, blanch these vegetables or cook them in slightly salted water.

Foods with a high fiber content.

If you eat too many foods, such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and legumes, it is highly likely to lead to increased flatulence. When gas accumulates in the in the intestines and their involuntary discharge, the smell around you will definitely not be pleasant.

Fish.

We all know that fish is good for our health, and we should include it in the menu at least once a week. But some people after the fish when you sweat the smell of choline. Choline is a chemical substance that it is found in fish and has a rather unpleasant smell.

In addition to fish, choline is also found in eggs and certain types of meat, which means that when eating these foods in large quantities, some people may feel an unpleasant smell during sweating.

Red meat.

In a study published in 2006 claimed that vegetarians are much less likely to have an unpleasant smell coming from the body. Therefore, it can be associated with the consumption of red meat.

The reason is that the body needs more time to process this type of meat and, in particular, what it contains protein. At this time, putrid processes begin in the stomach and intestines, which, in turn, are accompanied by bad breath and sweating.

Fast food.

Most of us like the food available at most fast food chains. But that doesn't mean it's useful. In addition to the fact that the in most cases, this food contains a lot of harmful fats, it is believed that refined sugar, which is present in large quantities, can also cause a bad smell.
